Welcome to Musical Ear!
This program aims to develop musicianship skills by testing your knowledge of intervals,
scales, and a few licks. The idea is to connect your ears and brain to whatever your
hands are doing on the instrument so you can learn to play and improvise freely. Note
that this program was intended for guitar, but I'm not stopping anybody from doing
whatever they want.

This is an open-source program, and my goal is to improve everyone's musicianship
without learning all the tedious (although it is still very important) music theory
concepts from a textbook, but rather by hearing the sounds in your head and working
to reproduce them on your instrument.

There are several modes:
* Random Intervals
* Intervals with Bank
* Random Intervals where you select String
* Intervals with Bank where you select String
* Random Melody (melodies, scales, licks)

If you are a beginner, you should probably start with the interval trainer. Whether it's
random, or banked, the interval trainer will teach you how to play (not JUST recognize)
the different intervals on your instrument, which is key to developing relative pitch
and also playing what you hear in your head. After that you will learn to recognize all
the different sounds including: triads, scales, melodies, licks.

To download:
* Grab the files from the Musical Ear folder
* Download the required libraries onto your device
* Run the program in your the console
* OR make your own launchers to make everything really easy
* Have fun!
